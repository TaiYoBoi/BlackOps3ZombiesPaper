TITLE

Limbo and Roland Barthes' "reality effect"

MEDIA EXAMPLE

COMMENT

You'd never consciously notice it, but look again at the very brightest portion of the background! See that shape that descends? This is precisely what Barthes is after--a detail that makes us find an obvious representation "real."

Barthes describes such images as follows:

QUOTE FROM THE READINGS

"the singularity of the descriptive (or of the 'useless detail') in the narrative fabric, its isolatedness, brings up a question of primary importance for the structural analysis of narrative"

CITATION

(Barthes, "The Reality Effect" p. 12).
